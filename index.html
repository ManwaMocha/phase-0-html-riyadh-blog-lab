History

Riyadh (/rijad/; Arabic;الرياض: ar-Riyad pronounced;[ar.riˈjaːd], "The Gardens" ) is the capital city of Saudi Arabia. It is also the capital of Riyadh Province, and belongs to the historical regions of Najd and Al-Yamama. I ts is situated in the center of the Arabian Peninsula on a large plateau, and is home to 5,254,560 people, and the urban cebter of a region with a population close to 7 million people.

The city is divided into 15 municipal districts, managed by Riyadh Municipality headed by the mayor of Riyadh, and the Riyadh Development Authority, chaired by the governor of the Riyadh province, Khalid bin Bandar Al Saud. The current mayor of Riyadh is Abdullah bin Abdul Rahman Al Mogbel, appointed inn 2012. Riyadh has the largest all female university in the world, the princess Nora bint Abdulrahman University.[2]It has been designated as a Beta World City.
